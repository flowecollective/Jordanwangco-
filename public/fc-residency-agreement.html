3. Program Description

The Flowe Collective Stylist Residency Program ("Program") is a structured three (3) month educational training program designed to provide licensed cosmetologists with advanced technical development, business education, and supervised salon experience.

The Program includes:

  • Technical skill refinement in a chosen specialty area
  • Business structure, pricing strategy, and career development education
  • Direct mentorship from Jordan Wang
  • Supervised salon floor experience with real clients and models
  • Participation in training sessions and demonstrations
  • Access to salon facilities, tools, and products during program hours

The primary purpose of the Program is education and professional development. Any services performed during the Program are intended to support the Resident's learning and are performed under supervision. The general structure of the Program schedule and training activities is outlined in Exhibit A (Residency Program Structure) attached to this Agreement.

6. Employment During Program

Concurrent with enrollment in the Program, the Resident is employed by the Company as a Salon Assistant.

As an employee, the Resident will:

  • Be classified as a W-2 employee
  • Be compensated at $7.25 per hour for all approved hours worked
  • Receive wages according to the Company's normal payroll schedule

Residents will record all required Program hours using the Company's designated timekeeping system. The Resident is responsible for accurately clocking in and out for all required Program activities. The Resident will be compensated at the hourly wage specified in this Agreement for all required Program hours, including training sessions, shadowing, model practice, clinic participation, and assistant shifts.

Employment duties may include:

  • Assisting stylists
  • Preparing color or supplies
  • Shampooing clients
  • Performing supervised services
  • Supporting daily salon operations

Employment status is distinct from, but concurrent with, enrollment in the Program.

7. No Offset Between Tuition and Wages

The Resident acknowledges that tuition payments and wages earned during employment are separate and independent financial obligations.

  • Tuition is payment for educational instruction and participation in the Program.
  • Wages are compensation for approved hours worked as a salon employee.

Under no circumstances will tuition payments or financing fees be deducted from wages or used to offset wages owed.

8. Training Activities

Training activities during the Program may include:

  • Shadowing senior stylists
  • Performing supervised services on models or clients
  • Assisting in color preparation or service setup
  • Observing consultations and service execution
  • Participating in training workshops and demonstrations
  • Completing assigned educational exercises

These activities are designed primarily to enhance the Resident's professional skills and experience.

9. Training Priority and Educational Acknowledgment

The Resident acknowledges and agrees that the primary purpose of the Stylist Residency Program is education, mentorship, and professional development. The Resident understands that any services performed during the Program are conducted for training purposes under supervision and may be offered at reduced pricing or as practice services.

The Resident further acknowledges that:

  • The Program is designed to provide hands-on learning opportunities
  • Participation in services or model work is intended to improve skills
  • The Resident is not guaranteed a client book, commission structure, or stylist-level income during the Program

The Resident agrees that the training and mentorship received through the Program constitute substantial educational benefit.

10. Resident Expectations

Residents agree to:

  • Attend all scheduled sessions and training activities
  • Arrive on time and prepared
  • Maintain professional conduct
  • Follow sanitation and safety standards
  • Respect clients, staff, and fellow Residents
  • Participate actively in the curriculum

11. Flowe Collective Obligations

Flowe Collective agrees to:

  • Maintain a professional and safe learning environment

  • Offer access to salon tools and facilities during training hours
12. Failed or Late Payments

If a scheduled tuition payment fails, the Resident will have five (5) business days to resolve the payment. Failure to resolve payment within this period may result in suspension or termination from the Program. No refund will be issued for completed portions of the Program.

13. Refund and Cancellation Policy

All tuition payments are non-refundable once the Program begins.

If the Resident cancels enrollment before the Program start date:

  • More than 14 days before start date: Full refund minus a $250 administrative fee
  • 14 days or fewer before start date: 50% refund
  • After the Program start date: No refund
14. At-Will Employment

The Resident's employment with the Company is at-will. Either the Company or the Resident may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause or advance notice. Termination of employment will concurrently result in termination of participation in the Program.

15. Workplace Safety

Residents acknowledge that salon work involves:

  • Sharp tools
  • Heated styling equipment
  • Chemical products
  • Close interaction with clients

Residents agree to follow all safety protocols and sanitation guidelines. Workplace injuries will be handled according to applicable Texas employment laws.

16. Confidentiality

  • Business operations

  • Proprietary techniques
  • Internal training materials

This obligation survives termination of the Agreement.

19. Non-Solicitation of Clients and Staff

During the Program and for a period of twelve (12) months following the Resident's termination of employment or completion of the Program, the Resident agrees not to directly or indirectly:

  • Solicit or attempt to solicit clients of Flowe Collective for competing salon services
  • Encourage or attempt to encourage any employee, stylist, or independent contractor of Flowe Collective to leave their relationship with the Company
  • Use client contact information obtained during the Program to provide competing services outside of Flowe Collective

Nothing in this section prevents the Resident from working in the cosmetology industry after leaving the Program.

20. Professional Development Disclaimer

The Resident acknowledges that the Stylist Residency Program is designed to provide professional education, mentorship, and hands-on experience.

Flowe Collective does not guarantee any specific outcome including:

  • Mastery of specific techniques
  • Income levels or earning potential
  • Development of a personal client base
  • Employment opportunities after completion of the Program
  • Business success in the cosmetology industry

The Resident understands that professional growth and skill development depend on individual effort, practice, and experience beyond the scope of the Program. Participation in the Program does not guarantee any particular professional result.

23. Chargeback and Payment Dispute Agreement

The Resident acknowledges that tuition payments are made for enrollment in a structured professional training program and represent payment for educational services provided by Flowe Collective.

By signing this Agreement, the Resident agrees not to initiate a chargeback, payment reversal, or dispute with their credit card provider or financial institution for tuition payments that were authorized under this Agreement.

If a chargeback or payment dispute is initiated, the Company reserves the right to:

  • Immediately terminate participation in the Program
  • Pursue recovery of unpaid tuition
  • Recover any fees, costs, or damages incurred by the Company as a result of the chargeback

The Resident agrees that this signed Agreement may be used as evidence in resolving any payment dispute.

24. Early Withdrawal

If the Resident voluntarily withdraws from the Program before completion:

  • Tuition remains fully owed and non-refundable once the Program has begun
  • Withdrawal from the Program does not cancel any outstanding payment obligations
  • If the Resident is on a payment plan, remaining payments will continue according to the agreed schedule

The Resident understands that Program tuition reflects enrollment in the Program as a whole and is not based on attendance or completion of individual training sessions.

EXHIBIT A
Flowe Collective Residency Program Structure

The following schedule represents a general example of the training structure used in the Flowe Collective Stylist Residency Program. The Program runs for twelve (12) weeks and meets four (4) days per week.

The schedule is provided for illustrative purposes only and may be modified by the Company based on salon operations, educator availability, training needs, client scheduling, or program development. The Company reserves the right to adjust the structure, sequence, or timing of training activities while maintaining the overall educational purpose of the Program.

Monday: Education + Technical Development
Structured education including: technical demonstrations, color theory and formulation, consultation strategy, pricing and business development, product knowledge, and salon systems.
Wednesday: Model Practice Lab
Residents perform services on live models under direct supervision to develop and refine technical skills.
Friday: Salon Integration / Assisting
Residents integrate onto the salon floor to assist senior stylists, prepare color and supplies, shampoo clients, and support daily salon operations.
Saturday: Emerging Artist Clinic
Residents perform services on clients under supervision during designated clinic hours. Services may be offered at reduced pricing and are intended for supervised skill development and building confidence on the floor.
Program Flexibility

Schedules may vary depending on salon operations, educator availability, or training needs.

Compensation Acknowledgment

As outlined in Section 6 of this Agreement, the Resident will record all required Program hours using the Company's designated timekeeping system. This includes hours spent participating in required education, Model Practice Labs, Friday salon integration/assisting shifts, and Emerging Artist Clinic activities. All approved Program hours will be compensated at the hourly wage specified in this Agreement.
