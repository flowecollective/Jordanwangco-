The Resident's Guide to
Securing Practice Models

Finding models is often the most challenging part of any residency program, but it is also the most important. The hustle required to find a model today is the exact same hustle required to build a fully booked column tomorrow.

Remember

You are not begging for a favor. You are offering someone a high-quality, supervised service at a heavily reduced rate. Own that value. Here is how to find your people.

1. The Warm Market (Friends, Family & Acquaintances)

Your inner circle is the best place to start, but you have to ask the right way. A mass text or a vague Facebook post usually gets ignored.

Be Direct & Specific: Don't just say, "I need models." Say exactly what you need.

The Ask
"Hey [Name]! I am currently doing a residency at Flowe Collective and I'm looking for a model this Wednesday to practice [specific technique, e.g., face-framing highlights]. Because it's an educational session overseen by my mentor, the service is [complimentary / only $XX to cover product]. Your hair would be absolutely perfect for this. Would you be down?"
The Ripple Effect
"No worries at all! Do you happen to have any friends or coworkers who might want a highly discounted [service] this week?"
2. In the Wild (Authentic Street Networking)

This is where you build real confidence. You are going to see people at the grocery store, at coffee shops around the Heights or Garden Oaks, or at the gym who have the exact hair you need to practice on.

The "Compliment + Pivot" Method

Approach them with genuine kindness. Never insult their current hair.

The Script
"Excuse me, I know this is so random, but I just had to tell you I love your vibe/hair texture. I'm a stylist completing my residency at Flowe Collective over on 34th Street. I am doing an advanced class on [technique] this week and I've been looking for someone with your exact hair type to be my model. It's highly discounted since it's a supervised learning session. Here is my card/Instagram. If you're interested, I'd love to have you in my chair."
Why This Works

It feels flattering, not salesy. You compliment them, offer value, hand them a card, and walk away without pressure.

3. Social Media Casting Calls

When posting on Instagram or TikTok, treat your model call like a professional casting agency.

Use Clear Imagery: Post a photo (even an inspiration photo from Pinterest) of the exact result you are trying to achieve.

List the Requirements: Be brutally specific so you don't waste time filtering through the wrong candidates.

Example Post
"MODEL NEEDED: Looking for virgin hair (no previous color), medium length. Must be available Thursday at 10:00 AM. Expect to be at the salon for 4 hours. Cost is $XX for product."

Use Local Hashtags: Use tags that actual Houstonians search for, not just other stylists.

  • #HoustonHeights
  • #HoustonCoffee
  • #HoustonBoutique
  • #HoustonHairModels
4. Setting Expectations (The Golden Rule)

Once someone agrees to be your model, you must set strict boundaries and expectations. A model is doing you a service, but you are also respecting their hair.

Time: Be explicitly clear that this is a learning environment. It will take longer than a normal service because your educator (Jordan) will be stopping you to check your work, adjust your angles, and explain theories. Tell them to bring a book or a laptop.

The Consultation: Before they sit in your chair, require them to text you a photo of their current hair in natural light, plus an inspiration photo. This ensures they actually fit the criteria for your curriculum that week.

Cancellations: Treat models like real clients. If they no-call/no-show, it derails your education. Confirm with them 48 hours in advance and emphasize how crucial their attendance is to your program.

The Ultimate Mindset Shift

Every model that sits in your chair is a potential future, full-paying client. Treat them to the ultimate Flowe Collective luxury experience. Offer them a beverage, give them a phenomenal scalp massage, and be a professional. If they love the result and the experience, they will happily pay full price to see you when you graduate to the floor.
